📚 About Kokborok Language

Language Overview

Kokborok (also known as Tripuri) is a Tibeto-Burman language spoken by the Tripuri people in the Indian state of Tripura and neighboring regions of Bangladesh. It is the native language of the Borok people and serves as one of the official languages of Tripura.

Historical Significance

Kokborok has a rich oral tradition dating back centuries. The language was traditionally written using the Bengali script, though efforts have been made to develop a Roman script for wider accessibility. The language plays a crucial role in preserving the cultural heritage of the Tripuri community.

Linguistic Features

  • Language Family: Sino-Tibetan → Tibeto-Burman
  • Speakers: Approximately 1 million native speakers
  • Writing System: Bengali script, Roman script
  • Official Status: Official language in Tripura, India
